If you have attended one too many funerals in Ghana, then you have probably heard “Where is our God in whom we trust” sang very solemnly usually accompanied by a regimental band. Did you know though, that is was composed by one of our own? I discovered this one Friday when I googled the song and expected to see a YouTube video with the name of a foreign composer but was met with the name of my own countryman.

James Hackman Tachie-Menson who passed on only last year at a ripe age of 85 was a Ghanaian captain who composed several songs at sea. He is known to be the first Black African captain of a ship. I felt very glad to discover a Ghanaian who wrote songs while sailing because that picture has never been a part of my imagination. Aside “Where is our God in whom we trust”, he has written other songs you have definitely sung in church before including the popular tune used in Ghana for “Hark Hark my soul, angelic songs are swelling”.
